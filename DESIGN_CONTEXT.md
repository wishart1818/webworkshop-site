# WebWorkshop Design Context

## Brand

**Name:** WebWorkshop

**Business:** WebWorkshop builds modern websites for contractors and local service businesses.

**Primary audience:** Roofers, landscapers, HVAC companies, plumbers, power washing companies, and other small local service businesses.

## Core Goal

The website should make business owners trust WebWorkshop immediately. It should feel practical, professional, grounded, and built by people who understand local service businesses.

## Brand Personality

- Clean
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Professional
- Direct
- Local-business friendly
- Calmly confident

## What To Avoid

- Flashy startup visuals
- Corporate stiffness
- AI-startup aesthetics
- Scammy sales language
- Overdesigned effects
- Generic stock-business feeling
- Vague promises without concrete outcomes

## Visual Direction

The design should feel like a polished digital workshop: precise, useful, and dependable. Use strong spacing, crisp typography, restrained color, practical layouts, and real-world business cues.

Recommended visual qualities:

- White or near-white page foundations
- Dark neutral text with excellent contrast
- One restrained brand accent color
- Subtle borders instead of heavy shadows
- Clear sections with generous spacing
- Professional photography or realistic trade/service imagery
- Simple icons only where they improve scanning
- Buttons that feel solid and dependable

Avoid dominant purple gradients, glassmorphism, decorative blobs, gimmicky animations, and oversized SaaS hero layouts.

## Content Voice

The copy should sound like a capable business partner speaking plainly to another business owner.

Use language that emphasizes:

- Better first impressions
- More quote requests
- Clear services and service areas
- Mobile-friendly design
- Fast turnaround
- Honest pricing
- Easy maintenance
- Local trust

Avoid language that overpromises rankings, sounds automated, or relies on empty phrases like "revolutionize your business."

## Main Website Pages

### Home

Purpose: Establish immediate trust, explain what WebWorkshop does, and move visitors toward pricing, portfolio, or contact.

Should include:

- Clear hero offer for contractor and local service websites
- Trust-building proof points
- Example business categories served
- Process overview
- Featured work or preview section
- Pricing entry point
- FAQ teaser
- Strong contact call to action

### Portfolio

Purpose: Show credible, polished website examples that feel relevant to local service businesses.

Should include:

- Project cards or case-study previews
- Industry labels
- Screenshots or preview imagery
- Short explanations of the business problem solved
- CTA to request a similar site

### Pricing

Purpose: Make buying feel straightforward and safe.

Should include:

- Clear packages
- What is included
- Optional add-ons
- No confusing enterprise-style pricing
- Reassurance around timeline, revisions, and ownership
- CTA to start or ask questions

### FAQ

Purpose: Reduce friction and answer practical buyer concerns.

Should address:

- Timeline
- Cost
- Revisions
- Hosting
- Domains
- Ongoing updates
- What the business owner needs to provide
- Whether existing websites can be redesigned
- Industry-specific templates

### Contact

Purpose: Make it easy for a business owner to start a conversation.

Should include:

- Simple contact form
- Business type field
- Project needs field
- Preferred contact method
- Reassuring note about response time

## Premium Template Roadmap

After the main WebWorkshop site is complete, create polished preview templates for these industries:

- Roofing
- Landscaping
- HVAC
- Plumbing

Each template should be strong enough to send as a live preview link to a real prospect.

Template requirements:

- Industry-specific hero message
- Services section
- Trust/proof section
- Gallery or recent work section
- Reviews/testimonials section
- Service area section
- Quote request CTA
- FAQ section
- Contact form
- Mobile-first polish
- Clear visual distinction between industries while staying within WebWorkshop quality standards

## Template Style Notes

### Roofing

Tone: dependable, protective, urgent when needed.

Visual cues: roofs, shingles, storm repair, inspections, before/after work, clean exterior photography.

### Landscaping

Tone: polished, fresh, outdoor, detail-oriented.

Visual cues: lawns, planting, hardscapes, seasonal maintenance, property transformations.

### HVAC

Tone: reliable, technical, comfort-focused.

Visual cues: technicians, equipment, heating/cooling, maintenance plans, emergency service.

### Plumbing

Tone: responsive, practical, clean, trustworthy.

Visual cues: repairs, fixtures, water systems, emergency calls, clean residential service.

## Implementation Priorities

1. Build the main WebWorkshop website first.
2. Keep the first screen focused on trust and clarity.
3. Make every page feel immediately useful to a local business owner.
4. Use real interface polish: responsive spacing, accessible contrast, consistent buttons, and clean forms.
5. Build the four premium templates only after the main site foundation is complete.
