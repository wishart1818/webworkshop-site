# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

WebWorkshop is for owners and operators of local service businesses: roofers, landscapers, HVAC companies, plumbers, power washing companies, and similar contractors. They are busy, practical buyers who want their business to look credible online without getting pulled into agency jargon, vague promises, or a complicated sales process. They are likely judging the site quickly from a phone, often comparing several options, and asking whether WebWorkshop can make their own company look more trustworthy to homeowners.

## Product Purpose

WebWorkshop builds modern, mobile-first websites for contractors and local businesses. The website exists to make a business owner trust WebWorkshop quickly, understand what is offered, see realistic preview work, compare straightforward packages, and send project details with confidence. Success means the site proves design skill through the experience itself, not through hype, and makes WebWorkshop feel like a real company capable of selling polished $500-$2,000 websites.

## Brand Personality

Practical, precise, and quietly confident. WebWorkshop should feel like a polished digital workshop: capable, direct, organized, and design-aware without feeling corporate, flashy, gimmicky, or like a tech startup. The voice should sound like a competent business partner speaking plainly to another business owner.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-startup aesthetics, purple gradient SaaS pages, glassmorphism, decorative blobs, fake metrics, fake testimonials, scammy urgency, overpromised SEO claims, generic stock-business language, vague agency fluff, and anything that implies client proof the business does not actually have. Avoid layouts that feel like interchangeable templates: split hero plus browser mockup, repeated card grids, tiny uppercase labels, generic benefit bullets, and bottom CTA blocks with no specific trust value.

## Design Principles

1. Prove design skill before explaining it.
2. Make the buyer feel safe before asking for contact.
3. Show contractor-specific judgment in copy, structure, and examples.
4. Be memorable through composition, typography, and detail rather than gimmicks.
5. Keep every call to action practical, clear, and project-focused.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, readable type at mobile sizes, visible keyboard focus, touch-friendly controls, descriptive labels, and layouts that do not depend on motion or color alone. Use reduced-motion-safe interaction patterns and avoid visual density that makes the site harder for busy business owners to scan quickly.
